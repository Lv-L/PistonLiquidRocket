# System Requirements

## Mission
- Bi-propellant liquid rocket
- Targeted for L2 certification flight (airframe integration, 640–5120 Ns total impulse)
- Satisfy [Tripoli bi-propellant liquid motor requirements](https://tripoli.org/content.aspx?page_id=5&club_id=795696&item_id=126571&)
	- Oxidiser: Nitrous oxide
	- Fuel: IPA, ethanol or 70% by volume IPA or ethanol mixture
	- Flight tanks
		- Aluminium, composite or composite-overwrapped aluminium shell
		- Pressurised only by vapor pressure of oxidiser
		- Passively depressurises in the event of a remote dump system failure
	- Remote operations
		- Non-commercial tanks employ a remote fill and dump system that can be operated from the minimum distance in the [Safe Distance Table](https://www.tripoli.org/safetycode)
	- Combustion chamber
		- Non-ferrous ductile metals such as Al 6061
		- Single-piece chambers may use injectors, nozzles, and nozzle throat inserts of graphite, composite, or non-ferrous metals
		- Multi-piece chambers may use appropriate materials for nozzles, injector bells, plates, and throats rated for expected pressures and temperatures
	- Oxidiser and fuel systems
		- All N2O components upstream of the remote fill valve must be N2O compatible and rated above max expected pressure
		- Fuel system components must be compatible
		- No active throttle control

## Cost
- Target budget: $3,000

## Schedule
- Design, manufacture, and test within 1–2 years

## Constraints
- Must be simple enough for a single person to build and operate
- Must be safe
- Rocket must be able to fit in my car
